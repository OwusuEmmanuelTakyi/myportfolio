## The queue that started it all

I watched a campus election at Legon take an entire afternoon because of a single paper ballot line. Turnout was low, results took hours to count, and students without smartphones were effectively locked out of digital alternatives other builders were pitching.

## Building for the phone people actually have

PrinPoll started as a simple question: what if voting worked over USSD, the same channel people already use to check mobile money balances? No app download, no smartphone requirement, no data bundle.

The web platform handles ticketing and live results; the USSD layer handles the actual vote for anyone with any phone.

## Where it's headed

PrinPoll has since been used for real campus events, and I'm now working on expanding it beyond elections into broader community decision-making tools — built for the infrastructure Ghanaians actually have, not the infrastructure pitch decks assume.
